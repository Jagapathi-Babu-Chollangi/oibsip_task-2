About

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on 2nd October 1869 – and died on 30th January 1948. He was an Indian lawyer,anti-colonial nationalist and political ethicist, who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific Mahātmā.

"If we could change ourselves, the tendencies in the world would also change. As a man changes his own nature, so does the attitude of the world change towards him. We need not wait to see what others do "

--- Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi.

Here's a time line of Mahatma Gandhi life:

  • 1948: On January 30th, Gandhi is assassinated in Delhi by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist who opposed Gandhi's stance on interfaith unity.

  • 1947: India achieves independence from British rule on August 15th. However, the country is divided into India and Pakistan, leading to communal violence and the displacement of millions.

  • 1942: Gandhi launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an immediate end to British colonial rule. The movement results in widespread civil disobedience and arrests.

  • 1930: Gandhi leads the Salt March, a 240-mile march to protest the British salt tax. This campaign attracts global attention and strengthens the Indian independence movement.

  • 1920-1922: Gandhi launches the Non-Cooperation Movement, advocating for Indians to boycott British institutions, including educational institutions, law courts, and government offices.

  • 1919: HGandhi leads the nonviolent protests against the Rowlatt Act, which allowed for the arrest and detention of Indians without trial. This marked his emergence as a national leader.

  • 1915: Gandhi returns to India and joins the Indian National Congress, a political party striving for India's independence from British rule.

  • 1893-1914: Gandhi goes to South Africa to work as a lawyer. During his time there, he experiences racial discrimination, which sparks his activism and commitment to nonviolent resistance.

  • 1888-1891: Gandhi travels to London to study law and becomes exposed to various social and political ideologies.

  • 1869: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, later known as Mahatma Gandhi, is born on October 2nd in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.
